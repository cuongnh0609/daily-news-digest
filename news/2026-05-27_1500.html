C1Hugging Face Publishes Definitive AI Agent Glossary: Harness, Scaffold, Policy, and the Architecture of Agentic Systems

📰 Article (English)

Hugging Face has published a comprehensive glossary addressing the terminology confusion that plagues the rapidly evolving AI agent space. The article draws sharp distinctions between closely related but distinct concepts that developers frequently conflate. At the core is the Agent = Model + Harness equation: the model (Claude, GPT, Qwen) is just the LLM with no memory or loop, while the harness is the execution layer that calls the model, handles tool dispatch, and decides when to stop. The scaffolding layer sits between them, encoding the behavior-defining logic including system prompts, tool descriptions, response parsing, and context management. Context engineering emerges as a first-class discipline — designing exactly what the agent sees at each reasoning step, spanning short-term memory (context window), retrieved knowledge, and long-term external storage. The glossary also defines sub-agents (agents spawned by other agents for subtasks), skills (reusable multi-step capability packages distinct from atomic tools), rollout (one complete agent episode from start to termination), and reward signals. For RL practitioners it covers environment, trainer, and trajectory. The article argues that imprecise vocabulary leads to architecture mistakes: conflating harness with scaffold, or model with agent, produces systems where the wrong layer owns responsibilities it can't fulfill reliably.

A sharp essay on the structural implications of next-token prediction as the dominant paradigm of LLMs argues that the technology concentrates power rather than democratizing it. While AI maximalists celebrate knowledge work as "solved," the author identifies a class dimension: AI raises both output ceilings and barriers to entry, remaining practically accessible to wealthy institutions and corporations. For engineers: this is a challenge to examine what we're actually building and who benefits, especially as we automate away the roles of colleagues below us on the economic ladder.
