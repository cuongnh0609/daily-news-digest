📰 本文 + 和越対訳

台風6号は現在、沖縄・奄美地方に向けて北上しており、6月1日から2日にかけて「強い」勢力で直撃する見込みだ。気象庁によると、沖縄では最大風速40メートルを超える暴風と、総雨量600ミリを超える記録的な大雨が予想されており、土砂崩れや浸水への厳重な警戒が必要とされている。さらに台風は本州へ向けて進路を北上させ、6月2日から4日にかけては九州・中国・近畿・東北など西日本から北日本の広い範囲にも大荒れの天気をもたらす見通しだ。海岸部では高潮の危険も高まっており、気象当局は早めの避難準備と安全確保を呼びかけている。交通機関への影響も広範囲に及ぶ可能性があり、関係各所は対応に追われている。

Bão số 6 đang di chuyển lên phía bắc hướng vào quần đảo Okinawa và Amami, dự kiến đổ bộ trực tiếp với cường độ "mạnh" vào ngày 1-2 tháng 6. C2SQLite Is All You Need for Durable Agent Workflows — Obelisk Makes the Case for Local-First State

📰 Article (English)

A May 29 post on Obelisk's engineering blog argues that SQLite, paired with Litestream for async replication to S3-compatible storage, provides sufficient infrastructure for durable workflow execution in most AI agent systems — extending DBOS's claim that "Postgres is all you need" one tier further toward simplicity. The core argument: workflow durability depends on preserving execution history and state, not on expensive always-on database services. SQLite's transactional guarantees eliminate network latency, avoid separate database services, and eliminate the operational complexity of provisioning and maintaining a hosted database. The architecture shines for AI agent workloads specifically because agents tend toward burst-and-wait patterns — heavy compute for a few seconds, then idle. Distributing small, self-contained SQLite databases across cheap workers in micro-VMs or containers offers superior fault isolation per task, lower cost at rest, and dramatically simpler operational models. Litestream handles backup asynchronously to S3 without blocking agent execution. The author acknowledges important caveats: async replication means very recent writes are not guaranteed durable if infrastructure fails before flush, and high-availability multi-reader scenarios may still justify Postgres. The recommendation is to default to SQLite locally and only graduate to Postgres when you hit a concrete scaling ceiling.
