A1飲食料品の消費税を1%に引き下げへ――外食・農水産業界への補助金支援も検討

📰 本文 + 和越対訳

C2KVarN: Native vLLM Backend for KV-Cache Quantization — 3–5× Capacity Gain at FP16 Accuracy

📰 Article (English)

Huawei's CSL research lab has open-sourced KVarN, a native vLLM backend that enables KV-cache quantization through a technique called variance normalization. Unlike most quantization approaches that trade accuracy for memory, KVarN maintains FP16-level accuracy while delivering 3–5× increased KV-cache capacity. The approach uses Hadamard rotation for channel mixing, iterative Sinkhorn-like variance normalization, and asymmetric round-to-nearest quantization. The default configuration uses 4-bit keys and 2-bit values with group size 128. KVarN is calibration-free, meaning no separate calibration dataset or pass is required — it integrates as a plug-and-play vLLM backend. The underlying research paper (arXiv 2606.03458) specifically validates performance on reasoning tasks, noting that KV-cache quantization causes error accumulation in long reasoning chains that KVarN's variance normalization technique directly addresses. Throughput equals or exceeds FP16 baselines while KV-cache footprint shrinks to roughly one-quarter.
