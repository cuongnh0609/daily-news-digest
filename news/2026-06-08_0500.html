C3Speculative KV Coding: Losslessly Compressing LLM KV Cache by Up to 4× Using a Predictor Model

📰 Article (English)

Fergus Finn presents a technique called "speculative KV coding" that achieves lossless compression of large language model KV caches at ratios of 2.4× to 3.9× depending on model precision, combining for 6× to 8× total compression on original BF16 caches. The method works by observing that KV caches are deterministic outputs rather than arbitrary data — meaning they are compressible via entropy coding if you have an accurate predictor. A small, quantized version of the target model runs in parallel as the predictor on both encoder and decoder sides, generating per-scalar (μ, σ) predictions that an arithmetic coder uses to compress residuals. The residuals are modeled as a three-component mixture: 95% standard Gaussian, 3% wider Gaussian, 2% empirical distribution for outliers. No additional training is required — the predictor is just a quantized (FP8) copy of the target model. Testing on Qwen3 models from 0.6B to 32B shows compression improves monotonically with model size, and the approach is provably lossless: decompressed output is bit-for-bit identical to the original. The practical implication is substantial: serving large-context requests becomes dramatically cheaper, and prefill cache storage costs drop proportionally.
