C2Computer Use Is 45× More Expensive Than Structured APIs — Benchmarks Force a Rethink

📰 Article (EN)

Reflex engineers benchmarked vision agents (computer use / screenshot-based automation) against structured API approaches on identical admin panel tasks and found a dramatic cost disparity. The vision agent required 53 steps and consumed 551,000 tokens, taking approximately 17 minutes to complete a task. The API agent handled the same task in 8 calls and just 12,000 tokens — completing in under 20 seconds. That's roughly 45× more tokens and over 50× longer execution time for the vision approach. The root cause is architectural: vision agents must process a full screenshot at every step and lack awareness of off-screen content, while API agents receive compact structured responses directly from application handlers. Reflex demonstrated that for applications you control, auto-generating an API surface now costs near zero engineering effort — removing the historical justification for vision agents on internal tools. The conclusion is direct: use vision agents only for third-party systems you cannot modify; for owned systems, structured APIs are the only rational choice once you account for latency, cost, and reliability.

The first law — Non-Anthropomorphism — warns against attributing emotions or moral agency to AI, as conversational interfaces are designed to feel empathetic and can mislead users into treating outputs as authoritative. The second law — Non-Deference — requires independent verification of AI outputs appropriate to the stakes of the situation, rejecting the notion that "AI said so" justifies a decision. The third law — Non-Abdication of Responsibility — asserts that humans remain fully accountable for AI-assisted decisions regardless of the system's autonomy level. The essay resonated strongly on Hacker News (263 points, 172 comments), with many developers noting that these principles directly apply to how teams deploy internal AI tools, write prompts for production systems, and communicate AI-generated outputs to stakeholders.
