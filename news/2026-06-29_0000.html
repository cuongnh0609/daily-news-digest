C1Prompt Injection as Role Confusion: New Research Reveals Why LLMs Can't Distinguish System vs. User Text

📰 Article (English)

Researchers Charles Ye, Jasmine Cui, and Dylan Hadfield-Menell have published a landmark paper reframing prompt injection attacks as a fundamental problem of "role confusion" in large language models. The core finding: models cannot reliably distinguish between privileged system instructions (wrapped in <system> tags) and attacker-controlled user content (<user> tags). More alarmingly, models appear to weight the style of text more than its actual structural position — meaning an attacker who mimics the model's internal reasoning style can override safety training even with models as recent as gpt-oss-20b. The researchers demonstrated this by testing "destyling" as a defense: rewriting injected text to break expected formatting dropped average attack success from 61% to 10%. While this is a promising mitigation, the paper argues it treats symptoms rather than the root cause. True defense requires models to genuinely perceive role boundaries — a capability current transformer architectures may structurally lack. This research has immediate implications for anyone building agentic systems that process untrusted input, from email assistants to web-browsing agents.
