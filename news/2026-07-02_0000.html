As organizations increasingly deploy autonomous agents, a critical problem has emerged: there is no reliable way to verify which organization operates a given agent, authenticate whether code or logs genuinely originated from a claimed agent, or discover agents at internet scale. ANS addresses this by creating "a distributed autonomous identity layer at internet scale for AI agents," building on DNS alongside Distributed Identities (DID) and Legal Entity Identifiers (LEI). Major technology companies including Cloudflare, Cisco, and Salesforce have already signaled support for the initiative. This positions ANS as a potential foundational standard for the emerging agentic internet — analogous to how SSL certificates established trust for web servers. The announcement arrives as multi-agent architectures become increasingly common in production environments, where attribution and trust verification are prerequisites for enterprise adoption.
