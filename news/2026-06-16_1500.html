The key insight Moussouris makes is that this is not a guardrail bypass — it is exactly what AI models should do for defensive security. Defenders must be able to ask an AI to fix vulnerable code, explain why the fix matters, and write tests confirming the patch works. That is the find-fix-test loop every security team runs. Banning a model from doing this damages the defenders far more than the attackers, who have access to purpose-built hacking tools that don't need to be coaxed through a multistep jailbreak. Willison concludes: "The prompts worked because they were defensive requests, and that capability cannot be removed without making the model worse at fixing bugs and verifying patches."
