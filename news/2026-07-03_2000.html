The Linux Foundation has announced its intention to launch Agent Name Service (ANS), an open standard that extends the existing Domain Name System (DNS) infrastructure to provide trusted identity for AI agents operating across the internet. As AI agents proliferate across services and enterprises, verifying which organization or individual controls a specific agent has become a critical security and trust problem. ANS proposes a distributed autonomous identity layer at internet scale, addressing four core challenges: authentication, trust establishment, governance, and interoperability. The system would allow validation of code generated by AI agents, operational histories, and chain-of-responsibility tracing. It supports integration with existing identity systems through Distributed Identifiers (DID) and Legal Entity Identifiers (LEI), rather than requiring wholesale replacement. Major industry players including Cloudflare, Cisco, and Salesforce have expressed support for the initiative. The analogy is clear: just as DNS gives human-readable names to IP addresses, ANS would give accountable, verifiable identities to agents. This is particularly relevant as agentic architectures grow more complex — multi-agent systems need to know who or what they are talking to before delegating tasks.
