📰 Article (English)

ServiceNow AI published a detailed post-mortem on migrating their PipelineRL reinforcement learning system from vLLM 0.8.5 (V0) to 0.18.1 (V1), uncovering four categories of silent correctness bugs that had been corrupting training dynamics without any obvious error signal. The most critical issue was logprob semantics: V1 returned raw logprobs before post-processing (temperature scaling, penalties, repetition filtering), while the trainer expected processed values — resulting in subtle off-policy divergence. Fixing required enabling logprobs_mode=processed_logprobs. Three additional issues were identified: V1-specific runtime defaults like prefix caching and async scheduling created train-inference mismatch; inflight weight updates required matching V0's mode="keep" and clear_cache=False during pause/resume cycles; and computing the final linear projection in fp32 (rather than lower precision) proved critical for accurate policy ratios, KL divergence, and gradient clipping rates. The central lesson: in RL training loops, backend inference correctness must be verified before applying any objective-level corrections. Conflating silent inference errors with legitimate off-policy adjustments leads to wasted compute and degraded model quality.
