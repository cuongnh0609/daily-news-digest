B01AIにルールファイルを数ヶ月自動更新させ続けたら、ルールは「良く」育ったのか——harness-entropyとharness-feedbackの実測

📰 本文 + 和越対訳

あるエンジニアが数ヶ月にわたってAIエージェントにルールファイルの自動更新を委ねた実験の結果が公開された。実験では「harness-entropy」と「harness-feedback」という2つの自動化システムを用いて、ドキュメントやルールの維持を継続的に実施した。分析の結果、AIが自律的に行う変更は大きく2種類に分類された。「削除系」の変更（古い参照の除去、重複の整理）はマージ率68.4%と高く、現在のリポジトリ状態と照らし合わせれば正誤を客観的に判断できるため信頼性が高い。一方、「追加系」の変更（新しいルールの提案）はマージ率58.3%にとどまり、実際に実装してみるまで効果の検証が難しかった。この実験はAIエージェントを用いたコードベース維持の実用的な知見として、開発現場における自律型AIの限界と可能性を示している。

Một kỹ sư đã công bố kết quả thực nghiệm sau nhiều tháng cho phép AI agent tự động cập nhật các file rule (quy tắc). Thực nghiệm sử dụng hai hệ thống tự động hóa "harness-entropy" và "harness-feedback" để liên tục duy trì tài liệu và rule. C02The Zero-Cost Fallacy: Open-Source Software in the Agentic Era — Who Pays When AI Generates the PRs?

📰 Article (English)

A Thoughtworks essay by Pamela Gay exposes a structural crisis in open-source software sustainability accelerated by the rise of AI agents. The core argument: the software industry has long operated on the comfortable myth that open-source is a "zero-cost public good" — but this assumption is collapsing under agentic AI pressure. The asymmetry is now severe. AI coding assistants (Copilot, Claude Code, Cursor) can generate pull requests to open-source repositories at near-zero marginal cost. But every PR imposes real review work on maintainers who are unpaid volunteers. At scale, one AI-assisted developer can now submit 10× as many PRs as before — each requiring a human maintainer to read, evaluate, and respond. The result is accelerating maintainer burnout, not from malicious intent but from pure volume asymmetry. Permissive licenses (MIT, Apache-2.0) compound the problem by enabling companies to commercially deploy AI tools trained on volunteer contributions without any reciprocal obligation. The essay recommends three structural shifts: treating open-source dependencies as supply chain assets requiring active stewardship; formalizing OSS patronage budgets (the way security or cloud are budgeted); and implementing rigorous Software Bill of Materials (SBOM) auditing for AI-era dependencies.
