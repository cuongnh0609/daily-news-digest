C2Anthropic Accuses Alibaba of Illicitly Extracting Claude's Capabilities via Distillation

📰 Article (English)

Anthropic has publicly accused Alibaba of illicitly extracting Claude's AI model capabilities through a technique known as model distillation. According to Reuters, Anthropic alleges that Alibaba used outputs generated by Claude to train its own competing AI models, a practice that violates Anthropic's usage policies and potentially copyright and trade secret law. Model distillation involves using a large, capable model (the "teacher") to generate training data for a smaller, cheaper model (the "student"), allowing the student to acquire the teacher's capabilities without direct access to its weights or architecture. This is not the first time major AI labs have raised such concerns — OpenAI has also accused DeepSeek and others of similar practices. Anthropic's complaint marks a significant escalation in the AI IP (intellectual property) battle, as frontier model makers seek to protect their costly training investments. The accusation comes amid broader tensions between US AI companies and Chinese tech giants over technology transfer. Anthropic has not yet disclosed what legal actions, if any, it plans to take.
