📰 本文 + 和越対訳

2026年5月のClaude Codeアップデートでは、大きく3つの改善が加えられた。まず「Skills検索ボックス」の追加により、`/`メニューからskillの名称や説明文をあいまい検索できるようになり、100以上のskillsを運用する開発者の管理負担が大幅に軽減された。次に「PostToolUse hooksの`async: true`フラグ」により、Discordへの通知や画像生成などの副作用処理をバックグラウンド実行できるようになり、メインのclaude処理をブロックしなくなった。さらに「HTTP hook機能」の追加で、hook完了時に外部WebサーバーへHTTP POSTを直接送信できるようになり、Buffer・Zenn API・Qiita APIなどへの自動投稿パイプライン構築が現実的になった。これら3点は、skillsやhooksの数が増えた際のスケーリング問題への構造的な回答といえる。

Anthropic's engineering team published an in-depth post on how they rearchitected Claude Managed Agents to solve three persistent problems in production agentic systems: stale harness assumptions, fragile single-container infrastructure, and network constraint mismatches. The new architecture decouples three core components: the brain (Claude + harness logic), the hands (execution sandboxes/tools), and the session (durable event log stored outside any single component). This separation means harnesses become stateless and interchangeable — a sandbox failure no longer destroys an entire session since state is stored externally and can be resumed via wake(sessionId). The design is inspired by OS abstractions — processes and files — that outlasted decades of hardware change by making no assumptions about the number or location of their compute resources. The post concludes that multi-brain, multi-hand architectures are now the default mental model for production agents.

Google DeepMind published a vision paper on evolving the mouse pointer — unchanged for over 50 years — into an AI-native interaction primitive. The core thesis: instead of forcing users to drag their work into separate AI chat windows, AI should meet users wherever their cursor already is. The team articulates four interaction principles. First, "Maintain the Flow" — AI operates within existing apps without interrupting workflow (e.g., hover over a PDF paragraph to get a summary, highlight a recipe to double all ingredients). Current implementations include Chrome's in-page Gemini integration, the "Magic Pointer" rolling out on the new Googlebook laptop, and Google Labs' Disco experiment.
